Introduction to Educational Outreach

One of the key insights which we have gained over the course of our project is that there is a general lack of awareness among the general public in India regarding the issues of climate change and carbon capture. This notion was seconded in our meet with a known climate activist who said that the single biggest roadblock for climate change is the lack of knowledge and ignorance of the general public towards these issues. Hence, we decided to make sure that we reach out to as many people as possible through our various educational outreach programmes to show them the effects of climate change while also showing how they can help and pursue the various career prospects in Biotechnology.

INYAS Session

About: We conducted the INYAS (Indian Young Academy of Sciences) session hosted by professor Arnab Dutta on 7th of June, 2024 in IITB Campus and this was based on CCUS and BECCS

We kicked off the session by introducing iGEM, explaining its role in fostering synthetic biology innovations across the globe. This set the stage for a deeper dive into the field of synthetic biology, where we explored its goals, applications, and how it serves as a useful tool in solving real-world problems. Our focus then shifted to the climate crisis, where we laid out the gravity of the situation, delving into the causes and factors contributing to this global challenge. We emphasized topics such as the carbon footprint, providing concrete examples to help the audience grasp the extent of environmental degradation. Following this, we introduced carbon capture as a potential solution to the climate crisis, detailing the various methods used, particularly Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS) and Bioenergy with Carbon Capture and Storage (BECCS). We critically discussed the advantages and limitations of existing solutions, ultimately leading into a presentation of our own innovative approach to carbon capture through synthetic biology. The session concluded with a lively Q&A segment, where students posed insightful questions that our team eagerly addressed. We also made sure to tell the students about career prospects in the field of Biotech.

Impact: By presenting the complexities of the climate crisis in a clear and relatable manner, we were able to broaden students' understanding of environmental issues and the potential of scientific solutions like CCUS and BECCS. This interaction not only increased awareness about the climate crisis but also fostered an interest in research and innovation among the attendees

SynBio 101

About: We designed and conducted the SynBio101 course, which ran from June 27, 2024, for four weeks. This course was designed to introduce participants to the exciting and rapidly growing field of synthetic biology, a field that allows for the redesigning of organisms for useful purposes by programming them to perform new tasks.

The course aimed to provide participants with a strong foundation in engineering biological systems, while emphasizing how these innovations can be used to tackle global challenges like climate change, food security, and human health.

The curriculum was structured to cover key topics over four weeks:
Week 1: Introduction to Synthetic Biology
Week 2: Fundamentals of Molecular Biology
Week 3: Genetic Engineering & Biotechnology
Week 4: Conclusion and Final Project

Participants dedicated 4-5 hours per week to engaging in course content, which included lectures, readings, and hands-on projects. Weekly quizzes, a mid-course assignment, and a final project were used to evaluate their understanding and ensure active participation throughout the course. Upon successful completion, participants earned a certificate.

Impact: IIT Bombay does not have a UG program for Biotechnology. Thus, this course was an effective way of introducing students to synthetic biology and iGEM. This also opened their eyes to the power of biotechnology in addressing complex problems, from environmental sustainability to healthcare advancements. Students not only gained a deeper understanding of genetic engineering and biotechnology but also developed critical thinking skills as they explored potential applications for synthetic biology. The course helped foster a sense of innovation and problem-solving among participants. The course was an effective way to bridge the gap between advanced scientific concepts and young learners, enabling them to take an active role in their biology-based interests.

Rural Outreach with CTARA





Our group of stakeholders for this problem was quite diverse, since climate crisis affects each and every person regardless of their socio-economic and geographic backgrounds.
The Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas (CTARA) is a body in our institute that focuses on helping technology and innovation reach rural communities and spread awareness about various scientific domains within the country. Hence we approached Prof. Anand B. Rao to understand the context of climate change within the urban and rural areas.

We wanted to know how we could conduct effective educational outreach sessions in the rural regions of India. The main issue that we identified was the language barrier. Since India is a multilingual country, communication in English is a tough challenge for many locals, especially in villages. Using their local languages, we will be able to effectively convey our scientific learnings and start fruitful discussions. Thus we decided to translate our outreach resources into local languages and make use of the diversity within our own team to help us increase the reach of our project.

Collaboration with NSS

NSS is a government sponsored public service program present in our college.
We collaborated with NSS and two NGOs named Vidya and Asha to conduct outreach sessions in schools in rural areas. These sessions introduced the concept of climate crisis and carbon capture to the students from these schools. The session was conducted by our team to children from classes 1st to 8th.

We distributed activity booklets to these students. These activity booklets had fun exercises and puzzles which were related to our topic of climate change. This was an innovative activity which engaged these students in our session. We have also made an online version of this booklet available in multiple languages. This booklet will be a helpful for future iGEM Teams and would be resourceful for them in conducting significant educational activities.

Cracking Carbon Capture - Talk



The team organized an interactive session on carbon capture technologies by inviting Prof. Arnab Dutta from the Department of Chemistry who works on bio-inspired catalyst design to capture CO2. In the session, he gave an overview of the climate crisis and why it affects each and every person on the planet. He talked about the various existing CCUS technologies and how they work and what their limitations are. To make the session engaging we also had a live demonstration of a CO2 capture experiment where alkaline water was used in which air was purged to dissolve the CO2. Then calcium carbonate was made by adding calcium ions to it. Students were also given a chance to work on the setup. This was followed by a QnA session to solve any doubts held by the students. The session helped the audience understand the current research being done and what challenges still remain in this field.

ITC WiSE Session



WiSE stands for Women in Science and Engineering.
Most STEM fields tend to be male-dominated. A prime example is our own college with a female to male gender ratio of 1:4 and it is necessary for us to make sure that women also feel comfortable in joining such fields. This was done through this session as it showed how there are endless opportunities. One of our team members, Kadambari Bhide held the session. Our aim was to motivate junior undergraduate females to join and explore more such opportunities in STEM. Examples of pioneering women in biotech were given for inspiring people while Kadambari shared her own insightful experiences to motivate the students. We also gave small fun demonstrations to create interest among the audience. In general, this also helped us create awareness among juniors.

Biology Crypt Hunt - Arcano



We conducted a biology-based crypt hunt event for undergraduate and postgraduate students at IITB, to foster curiosity and enthusiasm about biology as a domain and how it can be integrated with technology to solve pressing current issues.

The questions consisted of a mix of wordplay and deciphering of images with their captions. We integrated questions related to the institute as well to keep it relatable. Overall the questions made were very thought-provoking and all the participants enjoyed themselves to the fullest, even asking us when something like this would be held again.

This was a great experience for everyone and an innovative and exciting way to generate interest among students in biology based topics.
