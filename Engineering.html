Calcification at cellular Level

Design

To understand calcification pathway right from uptake to deposition, we decided to implement it by taking inspiration of different parts of the pathway from different analogous processes. The process was designed taking into account uptake of calcium ions, buffering by calmodulin protein, formation of coccoliths inside CV and deposition on the GPA protein layer around the cell . It was designed in order to understand the change in overall calcification and thus carbon capture based on changes in external calcium concentration, ambient (non-limiting) deposition environment and buffering protein- calmodulin.

Build

The process built took into account uptake across the membrane whose transmission rate depends on concentration gradient and activation of uptake channels by free calmodulin molecules. Apart from the calcium used for the cytosolic functions, the rest is bound to free calmodulin. The bound calmodulin is unloaded at CV inside which the formation of coccolith is mainly regulated by changing internal CV pH .The pH of CV determines rate of deposition by influencing amount of inhibitors for coccolith deposition on nucleation sites. Bone mineralization was used as an inspiration for deposition part of the pathway which was implemented using differential equations governing nucleation, GPA formation, calcification rate and changing inhibitor concentration.

Test

When the external calcium concentration falls below a limit, the calcite deposition stops by reducing the pH inside the CV which corelates with literature. At 10 mM calcium concentration (sea water) value of calcification rate from this model corelates with observed value and optimal pH inside CV is also achieved. It is also shown that GPA and corresponding nucleation sites is not limiting rather uptake which is regulated by free calmodulin is limiting. Thereby improved calcification rates is shown by increasing amount of free calmodulin

Build

Deposition is not limiting rather uptake regulated by calmodulin is limiting step. External calcium concentration can't be increased beyond a point since if internal free cytosolic calcium increases due to it, will lead to cytotoxicity thus increasing amount of calmodulin expressed improves uptake as well as buffering - increasing overall calcification and carbon capture.

Population Model of Emilania Huxleyi

Design

We wanted to know what are the factors that are a problem to the growth and development of Emilania Huxleyi in the oceanic setting (particularly the region where the temperature for the growth is suitable). So we designed a population model (inspired by previous mesocosm and phytoplankton models) with full efforts to include all the factors that can impact the concerned coccolithophore.

The model was designed to include ciliates, grazers, and other phytoplanktons (like diatoms, dinoflagellates, etc. that are prominent in the NE Atlantic). It also incorporates nutrients including nitrate, phosphate, ammonia, dissolved organic carbon, and silicates. Other necessary factors like the annual variation of irradiance and temperature were also included.

Build

Limitation due to nutrients, temperature and sunlight were incorporated using the Michaelis–Menten equation. Grazers were broadly characterized into ciliates, hetero flagellates and mesograzers along with bacteria. Which grazer eats which phytoplankton was properly taken care of. Variation of light intensity over the depth of ocean was taken care of using an exponentially decaying intensity function. Annual variation of mixed layer depth was input to the model. Similarly for the surface intensity and temperature. The mixing that happens between the mixed layer and the deep layer was modeled using the sinking of nutrients and organisms which is proportional to the difference in cell density. Finally free coccoliths and attached coccoliths were separately modeled.

Test

The Eulerian model provides results for all variables (population, net carbon capture, nutrient concentrations, etc.) as a function of time over one year.

We obtained population curves for E. Huxleyi, showing two blooms (for 47 N latitude), one large and one small, which aligns with actual blooming patterns. We adjusted nitrate concentrations and observed a significant impact on E. Huxleyi's population. This level of improvement was not noticed for other organisms, demonstrating the critical nature of nitrate for E. Huxleyi's growth.

Conversely, modifying phosphate concentrations did not result in a noticeable difference. Testing different half-saturation constants for nitrate showed a gradient of net carbon capture, offering a direction for organism modification. We also found that the nutrient concentration's effect on calcification rates per cell was not properly modeled, leading to unrealistic calcification rates compared to growth rates.

Learn

We learned that to make accurate qualitative and quantitative statements about carbon capture, we needed to correct the model regarding the nutrient impact on calcification rates. Supported by experimental data and other theoretical models, we re-modeled nutrient limitation using the Michaelis–Menten equation (initially, nutrient concentration only impacted calcification indirectly via cell density).

After modification, the ratio of carbon distribution between photosynthesis and calcification became consistent. We again calculated the gradient of net carbon capture concerning the half-saturation constant for nitrate, which remained significant.

Key Equation

The equation was adjusted by adding a multiplicative factor for nutrient limitation (phosphate and nitrate) in the first term.

PCR Amplification of Calmodulin

The gene of interest (Calmodulin) was amplified using Polymerase chain reaction (PCR). The gene of interest was synthesized from Integrated DNA Technologies (IDT). The appropriate annealing temperature was confirmed by the gradient PCR. The temperature with maximum amplification of the Calmodulin (CAM) gene was used to amplify the gene.

Design

The aim was to amplify our gene of interest, CAM, which would be used for cloning into the pET28a vector backbone.

Build

The gradient PCR from 47 - 65 ℃ was performed using TaKaRa PrimeSTAR® Max DNA Polymerase, with the parameters according to the manufacturer’s protocol. The synthesized CAM received from IDT was used as a template, and primers were used at a concentration of 0.5 µM. The PCR products obtained from gradient PCR were analyzed on a 1 % agarose gel following the standard protocol. The temperature yielding the best amplification was selected for the next round of PCR using the same set of primers for downstream applications.

Test

The PCR amplification was observed for the CAM gene (~500 bp) corresponding to the reference 1Kb DNA ladder (GeneRuler 1kb DNA ladder,Thermo Scientific) with no non-specific bands, confirming the accuracy of the amplification process.

Learn

The gene product was successfully amplified at an optimum annealing temp of 55°C. The amplified product can be used in the downstream cloning procedure.

Cloning of Calmodulin in pET28a

Cycle 1: Restriction digestion

Design

The aim was to clone our gene of interest, CAM, into the pET28a vector, following its amplification. To ensure the successful insertion of the CAM gene in pET28a, NcoI, and HindIII restriction sites were inserted at the 5’ end and 3’ end with the help of forward and reverse primers respectively. The amplified product and pET28a were digested with NcoI and HindIII. The selection of restriction sites allows the presence of 6x His Tag at the C-terminal of recombinant protein and would help in protein purification. The tag allows the binding of recombinant protein to the Ni-NTA complex conjugated to Sepharose beads.

Build

The pET28a and the amplified CAM were digested with restriction enzymes, NcoI and HindIII in two separate reactions and incubated for 1.5 hours at 37 °C. The digestion condition was according to the manufacturer protocol (refer Thermo). After digestion, the double-digested products were purified and ligated using the T4 ligase enzyme. The ligated product was transformed into XL1-blue competent cells. XL1-blue cells would act as plasmid maintenance strain. Five colonies were inoculated into fresh LB broth media along with proper anti-biotics which would help in the growth of the transformed colonies. Plasmid was isolated from the inoculated culture and tested for insert release using double digestion.

Test

After the restriction digestion to check the insert release, 1% agarose gel was run. The expected fragment sizes were ~5.4 kb for the linearized vector and ~500 bp for the insert. Also, ten more colonies were screened for the presence of the CAM gene using PCR amplification using appropriate primers and individual colonies.

Learn

Only a single band corresponding to the vector size was observed. No bands were seen at the expected size as of insert in either of the five colonies used to check for positive colonies. Also, no amplification was observed corresponding to the insert length size. We speculate that the reason for the no detection of the insert, either by double digestion or colony PCR could be partial or improper digestion and due to which vector backbone is self-ligating and getting selected on antibiotic selection plates. So we thought of proceeding with a different cloning strategy.

Cycle 2: Cloning of Calmodulin gene using Infusion Kit

Design

Since the cloning strategy using double-digestion did not yield any positive clones. Therefore, we proceeded with a more efficient approach, infusion cloning. The strategy includes linearizing the plasmid vector and amplifying the primers with homologous sequences to the ends of the linearized vector. Then the incubation of linearized vector and amplified PCR product with the required amount of the reaction buffer provided with the kit. The benefit of using an infusion cloning kit over other methods is that it requires only 10-15 bp homologous sequences in primers for PCR as compared to 30-40 bp in other similar cloning methods.

Build

For In-fusion cloning( In-FusionⓇ HD cloning kit, TaKaRa bio), we linearized the pET28a vector with NcoI and HindIII and also amplified the Calmodulin gene with primers containing the homologous sequences to the ends of the linearized vector. The 20µL reaction mixture was prepared using the reaction buffer provided with the kit and the reaction mixture was incubated at 500C for 30 minutes. The reaction mixture was used to transform into the XL1-blue cells and the colonies were inoculated into the LB broth media and plasmids were isolated and checked for the presence of the Calmodulin gene using PCR amplification, using plasmids as template and appropriate primers.

Test

PCR-amplified products were run on 1% agarose gel to identify the true recombinant plasmids. We were able to observe an amplified band adjacent to the size of the insert (Calmodulin gene) for each of the five isolated plasmids from different colonies. Additionally, we got the plasmid sequenced (from Eurofins Scientific) to check for any point mutation or frameshift mutation and also to ensure the accurate insertion and correct orientation of the insert.

Learn

The Calmodulin gene was successfully cloned in a plasmid vector and the sequence was confirmed using Sanger sequencing of the cloned plasmid. The true recombinant plasmid was used for the transformation of the expression strain of E. coli (BL21-DE3 RIPL).

Check the expression of the Calmodulin protein

Cycle 1: Expression in BL21-DE3 RIPL cells

BL21-CodonPlus strains are engineered to contain extra copies of genes that encode the tRNAs that most frequently limit the translation of heterologous proteins in E. coli. They are ideal for difficult protein expression, especially when codon bias is a problem. RIPL stands for Arginine, Isoleucine, Proline, and Leucine.

Design

The plasmid confirmed to contain the correctly inserted Calmodulin gene was used to transform BL21-DE3 RIPL competent cells by heat shock method and plated over the antibiotic selection plate.

Build

BL21-DE3 RIPL competent cells were freshly prepared and were used for the transformation. BL21-DE3 RIPL cells have antibiotic resistance against Chloramphenicol while pET28a has resistance against Kanamycin. 50ng of plasmid DNA was used for transformation by heat shock method and after recovery, the pellet was resuspended in resuspended in 100µL media. 50 µL of this suspension was plated over LB agar plates with Chloramphenicol and Kanamycin. Three different colonies were picked and inoculated into LB broth media with desired antibiotics added to it. 1% of primary inoculum was added to 50mL LB broth media and after growth at OD600 of 0.6, the culture was induced with 0.5mM IPTG and incubated at 300C for 4 hours. Culture was pelleted down by centrifugation at 6000xg for 10 minutes. Supernatant was discarded and the pellet was resuspended in 5mL of lysis buffer. The bacterial lysate was sonicated at 40% amplitude and pulse of 2 sec ON 2 sec OFF and total time of 5 minutes. The lysate was centrifuged at 14000xg to separate the supernatant containing solubilized protein and the pellet contained mostly membrane and insoluble protein.

Test

After transformation, the colonies were observed on LB agar plates with the antibiotics used for the selection of the true transformant. SDS-Page gel was run to visualize the recombinant protein. We were not able to see any distinct band corresponding to the size of the Calmodulin protein in the lane with induced lysate loaded as compared to the uninduced. The standard ladder with a range from 10kDa to 180kDa was run, acting as a reference for the molecular size of the protein.

Learn

As there was no distinct band corresponding to the size of the target protein, the reason could be either low expression or no expression. To test the low expression, we thought to proceed with the western blotting in the next cycle.

Cycle 2: Western blot to check the Expression in BL21-DE3 RIPL cells

A Western blot, also known as protein immunoblot, is a laboratory technique used to detect specific proteins in a complex mixture of proteins.

Design

Proteins are separated by size using gel electrophoresis (SDS-PAGE). The separated proteins are transferred (blotted) onto a membrane (PVDF) using an electric current. The membrane is exposed to an antibody specifically designed to recognize and bind to the target protein. The bound antibody is detected using a secondary antibody labeled with a chromogenic probe.

Build

The SDS-Page gel was run using the samples prepared in the previous step. The gel was washed with water and the transfer assembly was made in the following series- Cathode-Blotting sheet- Gel- PVDF-Blotting sheet- Anode. The transfer of protein bands from gel to PVDF membrane was carried at a constant current of 250mA for 30 minutes. The PVDF membrane was incubated with blocking solution for 2 hours washed 3 times and then incubated with primary antibody (anti-His antibody) for 2 hours and then again washed and then finally incubated with secondary antibody for 1 hour. The secondary antibody in this case is HRP conjugated.

Test

To check the presence of the target protein which contains 6x His tag at its C-terminal, the primary antibody used would be able to bind to the 6x His peptide, and the secondary antibody is HRP conjugated. The blot was developed by applying the solution of peroxide and luminol as a chemiluminescence method. However, after development, no bands were observed in the entire blot sheet whereas the control lane showed a highly enhanced signal. This confirms that there was no expression of Calmodulin protein in BL21-DE3 RIPL cells.

Purification of protein from large culture volume

Affinity purification

Design

Affinity purification, also known as affinity chromatography, is a powerful technique used to separate and purify specific proteins or protein complexes from complex mixtures. It relies on the specific and reversible binding of a protein to a matrix-bound ligand.

Build

For large volume culture, 1 liter of LB broth media shall be inoculated using 1% inoculum from overnight grown primary culture. The culture shall be grown at 370C with constant shaking at 150 RPM and induced once the OD600 of the culture reaches 0.6. After induction, the cells shall be lysed to release soluble proteins. Then the lysate shall be passed over a nickel-nitrilotriacetic acid (Ni-NTA) resin, which will specifically bind the histidine-tagged protein while allowing impurities to wash away. The target protein shall be eluted using imidazole, which shall compete with the histidine residues for binding.

After the restriction digestion to check the insert release, 1% agarose gel was run. The expected fragment sizes were ~5.4 kb for the linearized vector and ~500 bp for the insert. Size exclusion chromatography purification

Size Exclusion Chromatography (SEC) is a chromatographic technique used to separate molecules based on their size. It works by filtering a mixture through a gel or porous beads with pores of a specific size distribution. The gel or beads act as a molecular sieve, allowing smaller molecules to pass through while larger molecules are excluded.

Design

Size exclusion chromatography (SEC) is a technique used to separate proteins and other macromolecules based on their size.

Build

The partially purified protein after affinity purification shall be concentrated and loaded upon the SEC column. Samples are passed through a column filled with porous beads, allowing smaller molecules to enter the pores and thus travel more slowly, while larger molecules bypass the beads and elute faster. SEC is often employed as a final purification step to achieve higher purity and to analyze the size and homogeneity of protein samples.

Biophysical Characterization of Purified Calmodulin

I) CD of Calmodulin

Design

As Calmodulin protein interacts with Ca2+, Circular Dichroism (CD) is a spectroscopic technique used to study the secondary structure of proteins and other chiral molecules by measuring the differential absorption of left- and right-handed circularly polarized light. It provides insights into protein folding, conformational changes, and interactions, making it a valuable tool in biochemistry and biophysics.

II) Fluorescence

Design

The fluorescence of proteins at 280 nm primarily arises from the aromatic amino acids, particularly tryptophan, tyrosine, and phenylalanine. When proteins are excited with ultraviolet light around this wavelength, these aromatic residues absorb the energy and emit fluorescence, typically in the range of 300 to 350 nm.

Build

We shall formulate a solution containing the protein of interest with different calcium ion concentrations. Measuring fluorescence at 280 nm shall be used in protein studies for assessing conformational changes and interactions.

Biochemical Characterization of Calmodulin

Check the affinity of Calmodulin with Ca2+ ions

Design

Calmodulin protein is known to interact with Ca2+ ions. To check this interaction, we designed a matrix-based column that would bind to Ca2+. When the purified protein is passed through the matrix, it binds to the matrix and elutes using a buffer with a high concentration of Ca2+ ions.

Build

Calmodulin will be purified from E. coli after expressing it as a recombinant protein. Initially, it will be dissolved in a calcium-containing buffer to maintain its active form. Phenothiazine analog 2-chloro-10-(3-aminopropyl) phenothiazine (CAPP) needs to be coupled to Sepharose 4B beads using cyanogen bromide as an activating agent. This immobilization process must be carried out under specific conditions to ensure the stability of the phenothiazine and the efficiency of the coupling. The beads will be protected from light and kept at low temperatures (4°C) due to the light-sensitive nature of phenothiazines.

NAD+ Kinase Assay

Design

Calmodulin (CAM), our protein of interest, is responsible for the transport of Ca2+ ions at the site of calcification, i.e., the coccolith vesicle in E-Huxleyi. The objective of our assay is to verify the activity of CAM purified from E. coli as a recombinant protein derived from Emilania Huxleyi. NADK (NAD Kinase) is an enzyme responsible for the phosphorylation of NAD⁺ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) to form NADP⁺ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate). NADK is activated through Ca²⁺-Ca²-dependent CAM signaling, enhancing the production of NADP⁺ and increasing the availability of NADPH.

Cloning of Calmodulin with C-terminal Flag Tag

Design

Since affinity purification using a His-Tag is not as efficient as a Flag tag, we thought of cloning the Calmodulin gene with a Flag-tag at the C-terminal for co-immunoprecipitation experiments.

Build

The pET28a vector was linearized with NcoI and HindIII, and the Calmodulin gene was amplified with primers containing homologous sequences to the ends of the linearized vector. The Flag tag was introduced using the reverse primer, and the stop codon was included after the Flag tag sequence. The reaction mixture was used to transform into the XL1-blue cells, and the colonies were screened for the presence of the Calmodulin gene using PCR amplification.

Co-Immunoprecipitation of Interacting Proteins from E. huxleyi

Design

The co-immunoprecipitation (Co-IP) procedure using a Flag tag involves lysing cells expressing the Flag-tagged protein and incubating the lysate with anti-flag antibodies immobilized on a resin to capture the protein and its interacting partners. The protein complex can then be eluted and analyzed through techniques like SDS-PAGE and Western blotting to study protein interactions.
